Residual2Vec - Debiasing graph embedding with random graphs

2019-02-11
Sadamori Kojaku, Jisung Yoon, Isabel Constantino, Yong-Yeol Ahn. NeurIPS (2021)
“Your friends have more friends than you do” is a mathematical necessity known as the friendship paradox. Beyond an interesting piece of trivia, it has substantial importance in graph embeddings: many graph embeddings learn the graph structure by following the ‘friendship ties’ (or edges), which preferentially leads itself to high-degree nodes. How does this bias toward high-degree nodes impact on the generated embedding? Here, we find an overlooked built-in debiasing feature of word2vec that happens to negate the bias due to the friendship paradox.

Contact tracing

2021-01-07
Sadamori Kojaku, Laurent Hébert-Dufresne, Enys Mones, Sune Lehmann, Yong-Yeol Ahn. The effectiveness of backward contact tracing in networks. Nature Physics (2021).
A potent tool for discovery and control of epidemic spreading is contact tracing. Contact tracing has shown its effectiveness in epidemic outbreaks including Ebola, SARS, and COVID-19. Yet, it has not been clear what makes contact tracing highly effective. We show that the effectiveness of contact tracing is largely attributed to two independent biases in the process of disease spreading and tracing contacts. The first bias arises when a disease is transmitted through contact ties.

Core-periphery structure of networks

2019-02-11
Sadamori Kojaku, Naoki Masuda. New Journal of Physics, 20, 043012 (2018) Sadamori Kojaku, Naoki Masuda. Physical Revew E, 96, 052313 (2017); Sadamori Kojaku, Mengqiao Xu, Haoxiang Xia, Naoki Masuda. Multiscale core-periphery structure in a global liner shipping network. Sci Rep 9, 404 (2019).; Sadamori Kojaku, Giulio Cimini, Guido Caldarelli, Naoki Masuda. Structural changes in the interbank market across the financial crisis from multiple core-periphery analysis. Journal of Network Theory in Finance, 4, 33-51 (2018)
Core-periphery structure is a mesoscale structure of networks, where a core is a group of densely interconnected nodes, and a periphery is a group of sparsely interconnected nodes. Core-periphery structure has been found in various empirical networks such as social networks, biological networks and transportation networks [1, 2]. Many studies have assumed that networks consist of one core and one periphery. However, networks may contain multiple core-periphery pairs. We developed an algorithm to find multiple core-periphery pairs in networks [3].

Constructing networks from correlation matrices

2019-02-11
Sadamori Kojaku and Naoki Masuda. Proceedings of the Royal Society A, 475, 2231 (2019)
Many networks of scientific interest have been constructed from correlation matrices. Examples include protein interaction networks, gene co-expression networks, functional brain networks, climate networks and asset graphs. In correlation networks, the edges indicate nontrivial correlations between the features of the nodes. A widely accepted approach for constructing networks from correlation matrices is the thresholding; one places an edge between two nodes if the correlation is larger than a threshold. However, the thresholding may induce spurious edges in the generated networks because a large correlation may be induced by a factor irrelevant to the node pair such as a global trend and pseudo correlations.
