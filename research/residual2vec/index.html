Residual2Vec - Debiasing graph embedding with random graphs

S. Kojaku, J. Yoon, I. Constantino, Y.-Y. Ahn. NeurIPS (2021)

[Paper (in press)], [Preprint], [Code], [Slide] [Interactive Vis.]

“Your friends have more friends than you do” is a mathematical necessity known as the friendship paradox. Beyond an interesting piece of trivia, it has substantial importance in graph embeddings: many graph embeddings learn the graph structure by following the ‘friendship ties’ (or edges), which preferentially leads by itself to high-degree nodes. How does this bias toward high-degree nodes impact on embedding? In pursuit of the answer, we find an overlooked built-in debiasing feature of word2vec that happens to negate the bias due to the friendship paradox. We demonstrate that this overlooked debiasing feature can be used to negate other types of bias, and enable us to control the bias in graph embeddings.
